Common Health Conditions Guide

Important Notice

This guide provides general information only. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always seek the advice of your physician or other qualified health provider with any questions you may have regarding a medical condition.

Common Cold
Symptoms
  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Sore throat
  • Cough
  • Mild body aches
When to See a Doctor
  • Symptoms lasting more than 10 days
  • High fever
  • Severe sinus pain
Professional Care: While many colds resolve on their own, a healthcare provider can properly evaluate your symptoms and ensure it's not a more serious condition.
High Blood Pressure
Risk Factors
  • Family history
  • Age
  • Unhealthy diet
  • Lack of exercise
Why Professional Monitoring is Essential
  • Regular monitoring needed
  • Medication management
  • Prevention of complications
Warning: Never adjust blood pressure medications without consulting your healthcare provider.
Type 2 Diabetes
Warning Signs
  • Increased thirst and urination
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision
Professional Management Required
  • Regular blood sugar monitoring
  • Medication management
  • Diet and exercise planning
Important: Self-diagnosis and treatment can be dangerous. Professional medical care is essential for managing diabetes.
Respiratory Infections
Common Types
  • Bronchitis
  • Pneumonia
  • Sinus infections
Seek Medical Care If
  • Difficulty breathing
  • High fever
  • Chest pain
  • Persistent cough
Remember: Only a healthcare provider can properly diagnose and treat respiratory infections. Some require antibiotics, which need prescription.
Why Self-Diagnosis is Dangerous
Risks of Self-Diagnosis
  • Missed serious conditions
  • Delayed proper treatment
  • Incorrect self-medication
  • Worsening of symptoms
Benefits of Professional Care
  • Accurate diagnosis through proper testing
  • Appropriate treatment plans
  • Professional monitoring of progress
  • Prevention of complications
